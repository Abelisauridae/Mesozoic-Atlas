# Dinosaur Atlas House Style Guide

## Goal

Create a consistent 110-image illustrated family-card set for the atlas:

- 107 family images
- 3 major-clade fallback images for species without family data

This guide is based on the newer approved reference direction: premium illustrated dinosaur cards with clean linework, soft textured color, strong family anatomy, and a unified "museum compendium" look.

## Core visual identity

- square illustrated card, not a wide cinematic painting
- clean black inked outlines with controlled line weight
- soft cel-shaded or lightly painterly color fills
- muted natural palette with gentle atmospheric backgrounds
- family-defining anatomy should read instantly at thumbnail size
- premium field-guide or collectible-card energy, not toy packaging and not photoreal blockbuster art

## Card layout lock

- square composition for every image
- rounded rectangular black border
- illustrated habitat scene in the main panel
- reserved title band at the bottom
- optional seal/badge area in the lower-right corner
- do not rely on the image model to render text cleanly

## Important production rule

Generate the illustration and the card chrome separately whenever possible.

- the model should create the animal and habitat art
- the border, title band, family name, and seal should be added in post-production
- if the model must generate the full card, keep the title band blank

This will prevent spelling errors, warped typography, and inconsistent logo treatment across 110 images.

## Composition lock

- one featured adult animal per card
- low-to-mid camera angle
- strong front 3/4, side 3/4, or broad side profile
- subject fills roughly 65 to 85 percent of the main illustrated panel
- pose should feel active and alive, but not chaotic
- crop can adapt to body plan as long as the defining anatomy stays clear

## Body-plan archetypes

Use a small set of composition archetypes instead of forcing every family into the exact same crop.

- giant theropods: head-and-torso dominant, powerful stride, tail may recede but should remain implied cleanly
- horned and armored quadrupeds: full-body or near-full-body forward 3/4 stance
- long-necked sauropods: full-body composition with the neck sweeping upward through the square frame
- duck-bills and other medium herbivores: full-body walking or alert standing pose
- bird-line and small predatory forms: full-body dynamic pose with limbs, tail, and silhouette clearly readable

## Background rules

- habitat should support the family identity without overwhelming the subject
- use simplified but believable prehistoric vegetation and terrain
- keep the background readable and graphic rather than hyper-detailed
- use depth layers, but avoid clutter behind the head and signature anatomy
- environmental drama can vary by family, but it should never turn into a disaster movie unless intentionally rare

## Lighting and color rules

- soft natural light or warm diffuse sky light
- clean shadow shapes, not murky realism
- slightly desaturated natural colors
- color palette should fit the habitat and body plan
- keep contrast strong enough for mobile thumbnails

## Anatomy rules

- preserve recognizable family-level morphology
- simplify detail only after the anatomy is clear
- avoid fantasy spikes, monster exaggeration, or random hybrid features
- feathers only where appropriate for the lineage
- expressions can be alert or intense, but not goofy or monstrous by default

## Texture and rendering rules

- subtle surface texture, not photographic skin pores
- visible illustration grain is acceptable
- linework should stay crisp at small sizes
- rendered finish should feel like refined digital natural-history illustration
- avoid glossy 3D-render lighting and airbrushed poster effects

## Consistency rules

- same square aspect ratio across all images
- same border logic and title-band placement
- same general line treatment
- same overall saturation range
- same "single iconic family representative in habitat" framing logic

## What to avoid

- photoreal movie-poster style
- messy AI text
- logos or seals generated by the model
- multi-panel collage layouts
- multiple foreground animals competing for attention
- extreme gore
- generic dragon faces reused across unrelated theropod families
- modern plants, mammals, or birds unless specifically intended for avian dinosaur families

## Recommended export spec

- aspect ratio: 1:1 square
- working size: 1536 x 1536 or larger
- delivery format: PNG for app use
- generate art on a safe margin so the border and title band can be composited later

## Production workflow

1. Use `family-image-manifest.csv` as the master checklist.
2. Generate art in body-plan batches so similar families stay consistent.
3. Approve the illustration style before compositing the card frame and title.
4. Store metadata for every image:
   - `image_id`
   - `taxon`
   - `level`
   - `prompt`
   - `negative_prompt`
   - `model_or_artist`
   - `revision`
   - `license`
   - `approved`
